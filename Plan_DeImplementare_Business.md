# Business Planning: AI AML System for Detecting Shell Company Networks

**Context:** Product foundation document (Pivot toward the Financial/AML Sector).  
**Recipient:** Antigravity Agents (Development Team / Autonomous Systems).  
**Purpose:** Strategic and technical alignment on the application's vision, the problem it solves, and the new target market (Banking & Financial Institutions).

---

# 1. Executive Summary
Our application is a next-generation Anti-Money Laundering (AML) solution powered by multimodal Artificial Intelligence. The system is critically designed to identify, expose, and report complex networks of shell companies used for money laundering, fraud, and terrorism financing.

The product primarily targets banks and financial institutions, helping them comply with increasingly complex global regulations while minimizing manual effort.

---

# 2. Problem Definition

**Status:** Urgent, with a high risk of massive regulatory fines for institutions.

## Financial and Criminal Context
Criminal organizations use networks of shell companies to hide the illicit origin of funds and introduce them into the legitimate banking system.

## Compliance Pain Point
Banks and financial institutions are **legally responsible** for preventing such activities. Failure to detect these networks can lead to **multi-billion-dollar fines**, severe reputational damage, and major operational risks.

## Limitations of Current Legacy AML Systems

### High False Positive Rates
Legacy systems generate thousands of false alerts that must be manually investigated, wasting massive amounts of resources.

### Manual Processes
Entity due diligence to detect shell companies requires hundreds of hours of manual verification through commercial registries, OSINT investigations, and address correlation.

### Isolated Systems
Existing AML solutions are difficult to integrate and fail to provide efficient real-time monitoring.

---

# 3. Proposed Solution

**Core Technology:** Advanced Artificial Intelligence and Multimodal Analysis.

Our application modernizes the AML process. The system aggregates data from multiple sources and uses AI to analyze patterns, relationships between entities, and anomalies, delivering a significantly superior detection system.

## Key Competitive Advantages

### Drastic Reduction in False Positives
Using advanced AI models for contextual analysis, the system filters noise and isolates only real risks.

### Real-Time Monitoring and Continuous Updates
The system continuously adapts to new global regulatory requirements and scans transactions and entities in real time.

### Seamless Integration
The solution is designed to integrate with banks’ existing compliance stacks, delivering innovation without requiring a costly replacement of core infrastructure.

### Institutional Customization
Algorithms can be adjusted based on the size of the bank, its risk profile, operating jurisdictions, and specific integration needs.

---

# 4. Target Market and Scaling Potential (TAM, SAM, SOM)

Although the technological core remains capable of identifying shell entities, the Go-To-Market strategy is now exclusively focused on the financial sector.

## Primary Market (Go-To-Market)

### Banking and Financial Institutions
Global commercial banks, regional banks, and credit institutions requiring robust KYC and AML systems.

### FinTech and Neobanks
Modern financial companies requiring agile, API-first compliance solutions.

## Secondary Markets (Scaling)

### Defense Sector
Vendor validation for compliance with international sanctions.

### Insurance
Detection of organized corporate fraud.

### RegTech and Supply Chain Management
Vendor auditing for large corporations.

> **Total Addressable Market (TAM):** The global AML software market, estimated in the tens of billions of dollars, driven by increasingly strict financial regulations.

---

# 5. Directives for Antigravity Agents (Next Development Steps)

To implement this AML vision, the team must focus on evaluating and developing the following technical capabilities:

## 1. Open Architecture for Integration
Develop robust APIs and connectors that allow our software to plug into banks’ legacy databases and systems.

## 2. AI Engine for False Positive Reduction (Risk Scoring)
Train the AI agent (Chain-of-Thought reasoning) to produce complex risk reasoning capable of distinguishing between legitimate complex companies and shell companies, mathematically and logically justifying its decisions.

## 3. Continuous Monitoring and Scalability
Infrastructure (asynchronous backend + vector database) must be capable of processing massive volumes of data in real time while instantly adapting to sanction list updates and regulatory changes (OFAC, EU, etc.).

## 4. Customizable Dashboard
The interface must allow financial analysts to adjust risk parameters and clearly visualize relational graphs of suspicious networks.
